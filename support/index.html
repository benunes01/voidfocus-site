Frequently Asked Questions

How does app blocking work?

On iOS, VoidFocus uses Apple's Screen Time API (Family Controls) to restrict selected apps during your focus session. On Android, it uses a foreground service that detects when a blocked app opens and displays a blocking overlay. You choose which apps to block before each session.

Is my data sent to any servers?

No. All your data (sessions, stats, cards, progress) is stored locally on your device. We do not operate any backend servers. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

What happens if I uninstall the app?

All your data is permanently deleted. We recommend using the CSV export feature in the analytics screen to back up your session history before uninstalling.

Can I use VoidFocus offline?

Yes. Focus sessions, app blocking, gamification, and analytics all work completely offline. An internet connection is only needed for purchasing or restoring a Pro subscription.

Why does VoidFocus need "Usage Access" on Android?

This permission lets VoidFocus detect which app is in the foreground so it can block restricted apps during your focus session. Without it, app blocking cannot function. The permission is optional — the rest of the app works without it.

Why does VoidFocus need Screen Time access on iOS?

VoidFocus uses Apple's Family Controls framework to enforce app restrictions during focus sessions. This is the official, privacy-respecting way to block apps on iOS. The data never leaves your device.

How do I request a refund?

Refunds are handled by Apple or Google, not by us. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com (iOS) or Google Play's refund page (Android).

The app is not available in my language.

VoidFocus currently supports English, Portuguese, German, Spanish, Korean, and Japanese. If you'd like to see another language, email us and we'll prioritize based on demand.
